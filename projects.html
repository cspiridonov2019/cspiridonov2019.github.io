DICE Club

Our DICE Club Website contains information on the club's mission, members, and projects. I created this website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, languages I learned this year in my Advanced Programming class. (This website is also made in HTML and CSS)

We also created an automatic Twitter bot in Python that tweets out random daily statistics from the World Bank, embedded below. Funnily enough, this code is currently running on one of our friends' Raspberry Pis that we won at last year's Hackathon.

DICE Club's most beloved project is our daily newsletter made in Python. It emails our members every day and:

  • chooses a random date, time, and place (from a database of reasonable options) for our next club meeting every Saturday
  • exerpts from a random probability-related Wikipedia article
  • tells a randomly generated joke (the most absurd)
  • recognizes a random member as a "Member of Great Quality and Excellence"
  • prints our school's lunch option for the day
  • includes a random probability-related quote in the post-script

Unfortunately, though, we haven't uploaded the project to GitHub yet because files in the project contain the email and password to some of our email accounts, and we are researching how to encrypt those files.
